# Professor Feedback and Responses

## Feedback on Abstract (Round 1)

### 1. Language and Framing

**Comment:** The "traditional frameworks" of tala structures aren't exactly the five jati-s. Four were mentioned (Chatusra, Tisra, Khanda, Misra) but **Sankirna was missing**. Better to frame the approach through versions (jati-s) of the **eka tala-s**, or use other structures from the **suladi sapta tala system**.

**Action:** Reframe the project around jati-s of eka tala (clap + finger counts determined by the jati). Include all five jati-s. Consider whether other suladi sapta tala structures (e.g., dhruva, matya, rupaka, jhampa, triputa, ata) could add to the project.

---

### 2. Subdivision Patterns -- Gati vs. Tala Length

**Comment:** Are "subdivision patterns" referring to **gati-s** (note-rates) or **tala length**? These are different things and the abstract was ambiguous.

**Action:** Clarify in the revised abstract. The geometric forms correspond to **tala cycle length** (determined by jati). Gati is a separate dimension to be layered on top.

---

### 3. Embodiment: Differentiating Tala Beats from Gati

**Comment:** If participants step on tala beats, how do they express gati? If they step on gati pulses, how do they mark tala beats? Need a clear multimodal split.

**Action:** Design the interaction so that **two streams are perceptible**:
- One modality (e.g., steps) for tala beats
- Another modality (e.g., claps, voice) for gati

This mirrors the cross-modal cognitive processes described in the course.

---

### 4. Demo

**Comment:** Looking forward to a small demo.

**Action:** Prepare a walkthrough of at least one pathway (e.g., tisra jati eka tala: clap + 2 finger counts = 3-beat cycle) with a clear physical layout.
