Shaping Time — South Indian Music

An interactive guide to the rhythmic world of Karnatak music
Kyle Thomas Ramos MUSI 4106 · Yale University
Spring 2026

Shaping Time is a fully interactive educational website built around the rhythmic language of South Indian classical music — specifically the system of solkaṭṭu, the spoken syllable practice, and tāḷa, the cyclic metric framework that governs Karnatak performance. The site is designed for learners and practitioners who want to develop an embodied, hands-on understanding of these concepts through interactive tools, games, and visualizations.

Jāti system: Tiśra · 3 Chatusra · 4 Khaṇḍa · 5 Miśra · 7 Saṅkīrṇa · 9
What's on the site
🤲 Learn the Basics Interactive
An interactive syllable guide with spoken audio, trikāla (three-speed) exercises, and an animated hand gesture guide for every tāḷa — covering all five jātis and eight common tāḷas.
⬡ Explore Tāḷa Visualizer
An animated polygon visualizer where each tāḷa cycle is represented as a rotating polygon. Multiple cycles can be layered to reveal the moment of convergence — sam — in real time.
🥁 Practice Metronome Tool
A tāḷa-aware metronome with eight tāḷa options, animated beat indicators showing the hand gesture sequence (sam, clap, wave, finger counts), tap tempo, and subdivision control.
🎮 Games 3 Games
Three interactive games: Syllable Match (identify jāti from a spoken phrase), Mōrā Builder (calculate the gap length for a valid mōrā form), and the Hand Gesture Guide.
"Solkaṭṭu binds spoken material together within the metric context called tāḷa, which is counted by recurrent patterns of hand gestures. The relationship between the phrases and the tāḷa is either accurate or not: if so, there is a feeling of confidence." — David P. Nelson, Solkaṭṭu Manual (2008) · Primary source for all site content
